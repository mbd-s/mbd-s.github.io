This is the internet home of Michael Silverberg, a journalist based in Berlin.

I write about design, tech, and the visual world for publications like The New Yorker, Wired, the New York Times, and Fast Company. I used to be a reporter for Quartz and, before that, the editor in chief of Print.

Here’s some of my work:

I wrote about the rise of design manuals and the making of the modern New York City subway for The New Yorker (dot-com).

At Quartz, I was the first to report on the CIA’s internal style guide.

At Print, I commissioned a book on the visual history of Vladimir Nabokov’s novel Lolita, and why it almost always has the wrong cover.

And in the New York Times, I wrote about the retirement of the Met's beloved admissions buttons.
