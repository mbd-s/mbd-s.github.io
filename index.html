This is the internet home of Michael Silverberg, a journalist based in Berlin.

I write about design, tech, and visual culture for publications like The New Yorker, Wired, the New York Times, and Fast Company. I used to be a reporter for Quartz and, before that, the editor in chief of Print.

Here’s some of my work:

I wrote about the rise of design manuals and the making of the modern New York City subway for The New Yorker (dot-com).

At Print, I commissioned a book on the visual history of Vladimir Nabokov’s novel Lolita, and why it almost always has the wrong cover.

At Quartz, I was the first to report on the contents of the CIA’s internal style guide, which includes entries on terms like regime (“has a disparaging connotation and should not be used when referring . . . generally, to governments friendly to the United States”).

And in the New York Times, I broke the news that the Metropolitan Museum of Art was retiring its beloved admissions buttons.
